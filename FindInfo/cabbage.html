About Cabbage


Although cabbage has an extensive history, it is difficult to trace its exact origins owing to the many varieties of leafy greens classified as "brassicas".The wild ancestor of cabbage, Brassica oleracea, originally found in Britain and continental Europe, is tolerant of salt but not encroachment by other plants and consequently inhabits rocky cliffs in cool damp coastal habitats, retaining water and nutrients in its slightly thickened, turgid leaves. According to the triangle of U theory of the evolution and relationships between Brassica species, B. oleracea and other closely related kale vegetables (cabbages, kale, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, and cauliflower) represent one of three ancestral lines from which all other brassicas originated.

Cabbage

Cabbage a popular winter vegetable, Brassica oleracea var. capittata, of the Cruciferae family; a native of Europe. Cabbage is rich in vitamin A, B, C and calcium. Seedlings of 25-30 days are usually transplanted in October-November; average yield is 60-70 m ton/ha.

Cultivation

The above-ground green leaves are typically long and tubular, and are also eaten. In Bangladesh Cabbage is grown in an area of about 86,429 hectares and total Cabbage production is about 5,89,410 m tons. It possibly originated in Western Asia. The pungency, which varies with cultivar, is due to a volatile oil known as allyl-propyl disulphide.

Cabbages can be grown on all types of soils. The optimum time of transplanting in Bangladesh is December or the 1st week of January. Cabbage bulbs reach maturity when plants cease to produce new leaves. The average yield is 4-5 m tons/ha. Tahirpur and Faridpuri are two important varieties; the bangladesh agricultural research institute has recently released one variety called BARI Piaj-1. Cabbage is grown in all districts, but Faridpur is the largest producer of Cabbage. It is used as a fresh vegetable; as a spice and as an ingredient of curry, sauce, soup, pickles, seasoning food, etc.
